Just one more thing...
 
I have been actively seeking a job in web design and/or development for the past year, with little to no success. I don't have any work experience, though I have made a few simple websites and blogs for others, as well as a few practice pages for myself. I am mostly self taught, learning only the basics in school, walking away with a certification for HTML. Since then, I have kept myself proactive in forcing myself to stare numbingly at hundreds of thousands of lines of code, and dozens and dozens of hours of lectures and video tutorials, as well as over 10 years of modding many computer games source code for no real reason except I could, or because they just didn't have cheat codes built in.

I am confident in myself enough to say that I know I am a dedicated, hardworking, motivated individual who will do whatever it takes to accomplish my objectives. If I can do that for myself, I know I can do that for my employer as well. I do not expect to get paid some obscene salary, I just want enough to be minimally self sufficient in the Bay Area, something around $20-$30 per hour would be more than enough to live on. I am flexible, the highest I’ve been paid was $12p/h, and that was just enough to rent a room, eat and have a phone.

I can't make promises, so I won't. I am not trying to be overconfident in my abilities to deliver, so I try hard not to say anything that could negatively reflect on me as a dishonest person. All that aside, what I can promise is that I am far more driven by the knowledge itself, than the money that it can make me. I don't care about all the fancy perks of the industry, nor do I care about the recognition, benefits or status, I only care about bettering myself by constantly learning something new. I hope that makes it a little easier to understand what kind of person I am, and hopefully help influence your decision making process in my favor.

Thank you for your consideration,
